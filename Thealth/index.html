Technology and health during quarantine

Since the beginning of the pandemic, the population has been forced to live in a “new world”, where we cannot leave home or have contact with people who are not our daily lives. In this context, "Home Office" jobs have grown exponentially, and with that, we are able to advance in many areas, in science and technology, bringing developments mainly in Health. Telemedicine was prohibited until April 2020, soon after the crisis due to the pandemic and the need of the population, Law 13,989 was sanctioned for its use, with this, the population does not need to go to the doctor's office for the consultation, thus avoiding physical contact and dissemination of the virus, if all goes well, Telemedicine will be completely released after the health emergency on account of SARS-COV-2. Other advances with the pandemic were the saliva tests, which identify if the user contracted Covid-19 from the initial phase, through saliva alone. To avoid contact with other people, the test can be purchased at pharmacies, done and sent to the laboratory for completion, it is a more advanced test than the RT-Lamp, which uses the loop-mediated isothermal amplification technique with transcriptase (RT -Lamp) to identify the RNA of the virus. Health has also developed genetic mapping tests to identify various types of cancer and cholesterol, with the possibility of checking the roots of up to 8 past generations of a person, all with a saliva collection.

Increase in vaccine production

Although much questioned by the population around the world, it is undeniable not to notice the production of Covid-19 vaccines in record time. The production of vaccines like Johnson & Johnson and Moderna were boosted in the US with President Joe Biden's declaration that things simply weren't happening fast enough. Before J&J-eat vaccines reach anyone's arm, it must go through a sophisticated and complicated process that involves multiple companies. The process doesn't happen overnight, but companies say vaccine manufacturing has advanced at a speed never seen before in the history of pharmaceutical manufacturing. J&J said it has been working with “urgency” to increase the production of the immunizing agent, but it is not easy, as this requires very particular skills and experiences. It is not a simple thing to produce a new vaccine, there are certain steps that cannot be accelerated and must follow a great deal of bureaucracy. During the pandemic, Catalent, with factories around the world, worked with more than 80 Covid-19-related compounds from 60 different companies to make antivirals, other treatments and vaccines. It did all of this while continuing to produce hundreds of other essential drugs to treat everything from cancer to heart disease. Knowing the demand, it is continuously increasing the pace of its production. The company accelerated the expansion of the Indiana facility by about ten months so that it could have the capacity to produce vaccines on the necessary scale. It can be said that amidst the chaos that these professionals found themselves, the impossible becomes possible in a society that seeks a way out of this pandemic, whatever the cost.
